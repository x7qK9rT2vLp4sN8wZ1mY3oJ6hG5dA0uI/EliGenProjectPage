EliGen: Entity-Level Controlled Image Generation with Regional Attention

Abstract

Recent advancements in diffusion models have significantly advanced text-to-image generation, yet global text prompts alone remain insufficient for achieving fine-grained control over individual entities within an image. To address this limitation, we present EliGen, a novel framework for Entity-Level controlled Image Generation. We introduce regional attention, a mechanism for diffusion transformers that requires no additional parameters, seamlessly integrating entity prompts and arbitrary-shaped spatial masks. By contributing a high-quality dataset with fine-grained spatial and semantic entity-level annotations, we train EliGen to achieve robust and accurate entity-level manipulation, surpassing existing methods in both spatial precision and image quality. Additionally, we propose an inpainting fusion pipeline, extending EliGen’s capabilities to multi-entity image inpainting tasks. We further demonstrate EliGen’s flexibility by integrating it with community models such as IP-Adapter, In-Context LoRA and MLLM, unlocking new creative possibilities.

Continuous Entity Controlling Demo

EliGen can achieve continuous movement of entities with consistent visual identity. The position of the "person" entity shifts progressively from right to left, with EliGen maintaining consistent control throughout the process. This demonstrates EliGen's potential to generate coherent image sequences, such as those involving continuous movement of entities through positional control or complex actions orchestrated via coordinated control of multiple entities.

Generations with Different Seeds

Variations in random seeds lead to changes in the initial noise input to the model. EliGen possesses the capability to control the layout, ensuring that each controlled entity is placed in the target position even when the initial noise varies. Notably, the generated samples also exhibit diversity, as variations in random seeds result in changes in the details of each entity.

We propose the inpainting fusion pipeline to assist EliGen in Image Inpainting tasks, designed to preserve non-inpainting areas while enabling precise entity-level modifications over inpainting regions. Our approach offers three key advantages: (1) preserving non-inpainting areas without quality degradation, (2) enabling precise control over entity position and shape for seamless blending, and (3) supporting multi-entity inpainting in a single forward pass.

Styled Entity Control with IP-Adapter

When comibing EliGen with IP-Adapter, we can accomplish the styled entity control task. By inputting multiple entity details alongside a reference image, we achieve precise control over both the characters and the environmental style of the target image.

Entity Transfer with In-Context LoRA

When combining EliGen with In-Context LoRA, we can accomplish the entity transfer task. Given the entity defined by the source image, we can transfer it to any desired location in the target image through EliGen's positional control capability, thereby achieving the intended visual identity. This has broad application prospects in scenarios such as visual identity design, product design, and virtual try-on.

Pure Text-to-Image Generations

Despite fine-tuning on an entity-level control task, EliGen retains strong text-to-image generation performance without entity conditions, demonstrating that training does not degrade the model's image generation quality.
